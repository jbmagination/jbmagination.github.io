How to play Mario Kart Wii online

Requirements

You will need:

Either a Wii or Wii U

An SD card with at least 1 GB of space

A computer

A Mario Kart Wii game disc

Chapter 1: Setting up your SD card

To start off, we are going to set up the SD card so we can install the Homebrew Channel.

1. Make sure your SD card is empty. If it isn't, you may want to put the contents in a folder somewhere (such as your Desktop).

2a. If you are using Windows, download the tool fat32format.

2b. If you are using macOS, open Disk Utility from the Launchpad.

3. Look at the list. Keep in mind what drives are listed.

4. Insert your SD card.

5. Look at the list again, and look for the one that wasn't there before.

6. Format it as FAT32 (or "MS-DOS (FAT)" on macOS).

7. Download the .zip file available here.

8. Extract the contents to the root of your SD card (it shouldn't be inside any folder).

9. Your SD card is now set up.

Chapter 2: Starting the Installer

We are going to now install the Homebrew Channel so that you can set up Wiimmfi.

1. Insert your SD card into your Wii.

2. On your Wii, go into Wii Options (in the bottom-left hand corner).

3. Go to Wii Settings.

4. Go to Internet.

5. Go to Connection Settings.

6. Select the connection you're currently on.

7. Change the settings.

8. Once you are on Auto-Obtain DNS, select No, and click Advanced Settings.

9. Put the primary DNS as 97.74.103.14, and the secondary DNS as 173.201.71.14.

10. Confirm, save, then run a connection test.

11. Say No to the system update.

12. Back in the Internet section, head to User Agreements (or Agreement/Contact), then say "Yes".

13. If you see a pony on a light-blue background, you have done it correctly.

14. Do not click "I AGREE" or "I DO NOT AGREE" and wait on that screen for 1-2 minutes.

15. If your Wii freezes or does not continue after at most five minutes, please restart your Wii and try again.

Chapter 3: Setting up Wiimmfi Competitions

The Mario Kart Channel used to have competitions that you could play online, however both services used were shut down. This part of the guide will allow you to keep playing competitions.

1. Wait 30 seconds, then press 1.

2. Continue, and install the Homebrew Channel.

3. Continue, then exit.

4. Exit to the system menu.

5. Start Mario Kart Wii from the Disc Channel.

6. When selecting your license, click the gear in the top-right corner.

7. Select "Add Mario Kart Channel", and click "Install".

7. Select "Nintendo WFC Connection" or "Network Settings".

8. Select "Message Service" or "Message Delivery".

9. Opt out from all channel messages.

10. Go back into "Message Service"/"Message Delivery" and opt in to channel messages.

11. Go back to the license select page, and go back to the Wii Menu.

12. Open the Homebrew Channel.

13. If you're on Wii U, and it bugs you, you can run the Aspect Ratio Fix app and stretch it out to the intended 16:9 instead of 4:3.

14a. If you're on Wii, run and load the Wiimmfi Competition Patcher for Wii, then exit. This only needs to be run once, and the Wii will automatically be redirected to Wiimmfi's servers for competitions.

14b. If you're on Wii U, run and load the Wiimmfi Competition Patcher for Wii U, then exit. You will need to run this every single time you want to play a competition, as the Wii U does not support WiiConnect24.

15. After 6 hours, the Wiimmfi competitions will be enabled.

Chapter 4: Activating your license

1. Go into the Homebrew Channel.

2. Start "Wiimmfi Patcher".

3. Choose your license, then go into "Nintendo WFC".

4. Your license has been activated. After seven days, you will be able to play online.

Chapter 5: Setting up CTGP (optional)

Lots of people use CTGP to play Mario Kart Wii online. You get 216 custom tracks alongside the normal 64, as well as 2 new gamemodes. Wiimmfi is supported out of the box. This part of the guide will show you how to set up CTGP.

1. Start "CTGP-R Channel".

2. Put in your language.

3. Accept the terms and conditions.

4. Install CTGP Revolution.

5. Say "Yes" to automatic updates and verification.

6. Install the CTGP-R channel.

7. Exit CTGP-R.

8. Launch the CTGP-R channel.

9. Continue past the time trial support pop-up.

10. Go into the Updates section, and update to the latest version.

11. Once everything is updated, insert your Mario Kart Wii disc.

12. Launch the game.

13. Once the game is launched, while this may seem pointless: Exit the game. Seriously.

Chapter 6: Playing Wiimmfi Competitions

We set up Wiimmfi competitions earlier-- here's how to play them now that they're set up.

1. Run the Homebrew Channel.

2. Open "Wiimmfi Patcher".

3. Choose your activated license.

4. Select "Mario Kart Channel".

5. Choose "Tournaments".

Chapter 7: Configuring OpenHost (optional)

If you want people to play with you, it may get annoying to have to friend everyone constantly. Even worse, you have a limit of 30 friends on your friends list. This is where OpenHost comes in-- people can friend you, even if you haven't friended them back, and they can join your rooms. Here's how to set that up.

1. On your computer, go to wii-homebrew.com, and make an account after you accept the liability.

2. Once logged in, head to wiimmfi.de and log in with those same credentials.

3. Select "Assign a console".

4. On your Wii, open either CTGP or the Wiimmfi Patcher.

5. Go into "Friends".

6. Back on your computer, type in the friend code at the bottom-center of the screen.

7. Once assigned, head here, and set your OpenHost mode to whitelist.

Troubleshooting

Having any issues? Read the answers below, or fill out the form if your problem isn't listed here!

Q. I'm getting error code 60000 when attempting to launch Wiimmfi!

A. Unfortunately, you may need to make a new license, or use the PID Tool. If you're seeing this, I don't have my Wii U with me at the moment, so I can't get you the PID Tool. However, in the meantime, you can make a new license.

Q. I'm getting error code 23899 when attempting to launch Wiimmfi!

A. That's perfectly normal! Wait seven days, and you'll be able to join Wiimmfi. Use CTGP to see how much longer you need to wait until you can join.
